Mathematical Knowledge Management

Session Organizers:

  • Ilka Agricola (Marburg),
  • Patrick Ion (Ann Arbor),
  • Moritz Schubotz (FIZ Karlsruhe),
  • Stephen Watt (University of Waterloo).

Session Abstract:

This session is about the opportunities and technologies that software provides to facilitate communication and development of mathematics and access to mathematical knowledge.

Topics (including, but not limited to)

  • What is mathematical knowledge and how can it be represented?
  • Software for manipulation, storage and retrieval of mathematical knowledge
  • Recovery of machine-usable mathematical knowledge from traditional publications
  • Integration of machine-usable mathematical knowledge in new publication
  • Integrating levels of formalism in the treatment of mathematical knowledge
  • The use of generative AI, computer algebra and formal deduction systems for mathematical knowledge management
  • Mathematical search (for definitions, results, explanations, etc)
  • Automated summary of mathematical documents
  • Automated classification of mathematical documents
  • Mathematical knowledge network navigation
  • Indexing and meta-knowledge
  • Rights management
  • Fraud detection
  • Authority in mathematical knowledge management
  • Collaboration tools
  • Curation and the life cycle of mathematical knowledge bases
  • Scalability of mathematical knowledge systems
